SWE Science


This Spring 2021 semester, due to COVID-19, all SWE Science Saturdays
and SWE Science Scholars events will be held online via Zoom.

SWE Science Saturdays and SWE Science Scholars are programs that teach engineering ideas and principles to 4th and 5th grade students and 6th - 8th grade students, respectively. The students will be able to complete various hands-on activities using their creativity and knowledge. Some examples of previous activities include creating catapults and building “earthquake-proof” structures out of classroom materials such as popsicle sticks, rubber bands, straws, and chopsticks.

Please save the dates! The program will be hosted 2/13, 3/6, 4/3, and 5/1 on Saturdays 10AM - 11:30AM.
All activities will be made out of common household items.

Can't attend the program this semester? We invite any interested parents and teachers to sign up for our Elementary & Middle School Outreach Mailing List to stay updated on future SWE Science programs.

FAQ

What is SWE Science?
SWE Science is a hands-on engineering program held from 10AM to 11:30AM on Saturday mornings. During these sessions, students will engage in engineering challenges designed to allow students to not only practice foundational STEM principles but also to develop their intellectual curiosities and creativity. Previous activities have included cardboard vehicles, flotation devices, and plastic parachutes.
Who can participate?
SWE Science is divided into two sections: SWE Science Saturdays for 4th-5th grade and SWE Science Scholars for 6th-8th grade students. We welcome students of all genders and backgrounds to attend.
